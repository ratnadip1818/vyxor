Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about position sizing and risk management.

What is position sizing in trading?
+
Position sizing is the process of calculating how many units, lots, or contracts to trade based on your account size, the percentage of capital you are willing to risk, and the distance to your stop loss. Proper position sizing is the single most important factor in trader survival.
How do I calculate forex lot size with a stop loss?
+
The forex lot size formula is: (Account Balance x Risk %) divided by (Stop Loss in Pips x Pip Value). For example, with a $10,000 account risking 1% ($100), trading EUR/USD with a 20-pip stop and $10 pip value: lot size = $100 divided by (20 x $10) = 0.5 standard lots.
What is the NQ futures tick value?
+
NQ (E-mini Nasdaq-100) futures have a tick size of 0.25 points and a tick value of $5.00 per contract. Each 1-point move equals $20. The micro version MNQ has the same 0.25 tick size but only $0.50 tick value, making it exactly 1/10th the size.
What is a good risk-reward ratio for trading?
+
A minimum 2:1 risk-reward ratio is recommended for most strategies. This means you target $2 in profit for every $1 you risk. With 2:1 R:R, you only need a 33% win rate to break even.
